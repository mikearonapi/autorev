# Top 100 vehicles by US modification market size

The Ford F-150 and Jeep Wrangler dominate the American automotive aftermarket, which hit a record **$52.65 billion in 2024**. Pickup trucks alone command **32% of specialty equipment sales ($16.8B annually)**, while SUVs/off-road vehicles represent **12% ($6.3B)**. Sports cars and muscle cars follow with approximately **$3.1B in specialty equipment**. This ranking synthesizes forum membership data, Reddit subscribers, tuning platform support, SEMA representation, and aftermarket catalog depth to create a definitive prioritization list for tuning shop application coverage.

## Key findings for tuning shop prioritization

**Highest ROI platforms (Top 20)** should be primary development focus—these vehicles represent the vast majority of modification spending and have established tuning infrastructure. The F-150, Wrangler, and Mustang alone likely account for **20-25% of total US aftermarket revenue**.

**Forum size correlates strongly with market size**: VWVortex's 1.6M members for GTI, Corvette Forum's 500K, TacomaWorld's 395K, and NASIOC's 375K for WRX/STI represent the largest concentrated enthusiast communities. These platforms have predictable, recurring parts demand.

**Tuning platform support is critical**: Vehicles with **COBB, APR, Hondata, or HP Tuners support** have dramatically larger modification markets than those requiring standalone ECUs. The Top 40 vehicles all have at least one major plug-and-play tuning solution.

**Emerging growth opportunities**: GR Corolla (10K forum members in <3 years), Veloster N/Elantra N (SXTH Element ecosystem), and Tesla Model 3/Y (33K+ mod forum posts) represent the fastest-growing segments that may move up this ranking over the next 2-3 years.

**Production status matters**: Discontinued vehicles (Camaro, Challenger/Charger HEMI-era, Evo, FRS/BRZ Gen 1, Focus ST/RS) maintain strong communities but see declining new parts development—prioritize current-production vehicles for long-term platform viability.
